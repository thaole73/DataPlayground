In this project, I used a sample Christmas sales dataset to help a retailer analyze customer behaviors during the festive season. The dataset includes information about Christmas sales across various product categories, customer demographics, and purchase behavior. By visualizing customer behavior patterns and product performance metrics, this analysis aims to uncover actionable insights that could help the retailer optimize performance during this critical sales period.

How I Prepared the Data

First, I had to clean up data by removing unnecessary columns and rows and standardizing data types using Power Query Editor so everything worked properly. Additionally, I renamed tables with simple "fact" and "dim" prefixes so I could easily tell them apart when modeling data. I also built a special date table that lets us analyze trends over time.

Next, I connected all the pieces together. The main sales table now links to country information, purchase types, and dates. These relationships established the cross-filtering pathways necessary for dynamic and accurate visualizations

I then created calculated measures tracking profit percentages, year-over-year growth metrics, and compound annual growth rates to quantify performance changes across different segments and timeframes. 

What's in the Dashboard

The dashboard tells the Christmas sales story across three simple pages:

The Sales Overview page shows the big picture at a glance. Total sales reached $28.53M (up 5.6% from last year) with profits of $21.94M (up 5.5%). The bard chart shows that Sweden, the Netherlands, and Germany were the holiday shopping champions. Swedish cities like Malmö, Gothenburg, and Stockholm each brought in over $1.2M in sales.

The Customer Analysis page reveals who's doing all this Christmas shopping. Surprisingly, products for children ages 1-11 drove the most sales, followed by teen products. Boys and girls had almost equal influence, with female shoppers at 50.57% and males at 49.43%. The page also shows when people shop most during the holiday season, which shopping channels they prefer, and how they like to pay.

The Pricing Strategy page turns insights into action. It shows how many items people typically buy in one order and suggests bundle deals to boost sales. It also recommends different pricing approaches based on product type and customer age - helping retailers know when to discount and when to hold firm on prices.

Key Insights & Recommendations


Sales Performance


  • Insight: Positive growth in total sales, quantity sold and total orders highlights healthy customer demand.

  • Recommendation: Leverage this upward trend by scaling marketing efforts and promotions earlier in the holiday season to capture more demand. Analyze contributing factors to profitability (e.g., cost control, pricing) and replicate these strategies across underperforming regions or categories.

Regional Focus


  • Insight: Nordic and German markets show strongest performance.

  • Recommendation: Focus marketing campaigns and allocate more inventory to these high-performing countries. Investigate customer preferences and shopping behaviors in these regions to replicate success in lower-performing areas. Strengthen logistics and supply chains in these regions to prevent stockouts during peak demand.

Provide flexible payment options and emphasize security to attract more digital payment users. Offer exclusive benefits, like cashback or loyalty points, for high-value transactions.
